PCB Trace Current Capacity Reference
====================================

Calculator: https://ptorrone.github.io/artifacts/pcb-trace-current/
Source:     IPC-2221, Generic Standard on Printed Board Design,
            Section 6.2 (External Conductors)


WHAT THIS CALCULATES
--------------------

The maximum continuous current a PCB trace can carry without
exceeding a specified temperature rise above ambient.


FORMULA
-------

  I = k * (dT ^ 0.44) * (A ^ 0.725)

where:
  I  = current, in amps
  k  = layer constant (see below)
  dT = temperature rise above ambient, in degrees Celsius
  A  = cross-sectional area, in mils squared

  A = w * t

where:
  w = trace width, in mils
  t = copper thickness, in mils


LAYER CONSTANT (k)
------------------

  External layer (top or bottom):  k = 0.048
  Internal layer (buried):         k = 0.024

Internal traces dissipate heat through surrounding FR4 instead of
air, so capacity is roughly half that of an external trace at the
same dimensions and temperature rise.

UNIT REFERENCE
--------------

  1 mil      = 0.001 inch = 0.0254 mm = 25.4 micrometers
  1 oz/ft^2  = 1.378 mils thick = 35 micrometers thick
               (standard plating designation, by weight per unit area)


CAVEATS
-------

The IPC-2221 formula assumes steady-state DC current in still air at
standard atmospheric pressure with a single isolated trace. It does
NOT account for:

  - Forced air or liquid cooling (raises capacity)
  - Adjacent traces in close proximity (reduces capacity due to
    mutual heating)
  - High-frequency skin effect (relevant above ~10 MHz)
  - Pulsed or transient currents (use I^2*t analysis separately)
  - Vias, which are typically the limiting factor in high-current
    designs and require their own thermal analysis
  - Solder mask presence (slightly raises external trace capacity)

For mission-critical designs, simulate thermally with the actual
board stackup and ambient conditions, or follow IPC-2152 (the
successor standard) which corrects several conservatism issues
in IPC-2221 and provides separate curves for internal vs external.
